The Knut and Alice Wallenberg Foundation is the largest private financier of research in Sweden and one of the most influential philanthropic foundations in Europe. Established in 1917, the Foundation’s primary mission is to support scientific research that benefits Sweden, focusing heavily on basic research in medicine, technology, and the natural sciences. Through long-term grants to exceptional individual researchers and cutting-edge projects, the Foundation plays a pivotal role in driving strategic scientific advancement, fostering innovation, and strengthening Sweden's position as a leading global research hub.
